Article 2 【Application of Terms】

Article 3 【Definition of Terms】

  • “Non-face-to-face” refers to the channels by which this transaction can be used when using the bank’s Internet banking, smart banking, All One Bank app, automation devices, and other electronic financial media, without using one of the bank’s branches.
  • “Remittance information” refers to the information required when applying for an overseas remittance, such as the remitter’s basic information, reason for remittance, the recipient, and the receiving country.

Article 4 【Description of Service】

  • This transaction service shall be arranged and provided in such a way that the applicant shall first visit the bank’s branch where he/she registers his/her remittance information so as to directly remit money overseas using the bank’s current non-face-to-face channels.

Article 5 【Customers】

  • In order to use this transaction, a real-name person shall designate the bank as the foreign-exchange bank or, when using an automation device, the user shall use the bank-issued cash card (including debit card) or credit card or card with cash withdrawal function.

Article 6 【Registration of Remittance Information】

  • To use this transaction, the applicant shall first register the necessary information with the bank using the bank’s prescribed form. If there is a change to the remittance information registered with the bank, the user shall immediately visit the bank’s branch to register the updated information.

Article 7 【Remittance Amount】

  • The maximum remittance amount of this transaction shall be a KRW amount equivalent to USD 5,000 (based on the remittance amount) per transaction and per day. The transaction shall be subject to the prescribed limit on money transfer by automation device and electronic financial media.

Article 8 【Remittance Fee and Exchange Rate】

  • The overseas remittance fee of this transaction shall follow the “Non-face-to-face NH - Western Union Autosend Fee Table,” which is available from any of the bank’s branches and the bank's website.
  • This transaction shall apply the telegraphic transfer selling rate announced at the time of the remittance (exchange rate when remitting money).

Article 9 【Change and Cancellation of a Remittance Transaction】

  • The remittance that has been made by this transaction cannot be canceled or changed once it's done. However, this transaction will be cancelled if the recipient is classified as disqualified for this transaction in the receiving country, thus disabling payment.
  • Notwithstanding the main text of the forgoing (1), when the applicant requests the cancellation of this transaction due to the recipient’s moving to another country (including moving to another state), etc., the bank will pay the applicant the KRW amount – which will be converted at the telegraphic buying rate on the day of payment from the amount in the foreign currency returned less all costs incurred after receiving the remittance cancellation certificate from the applicant, if the remitted money cannot be paid to the overseas recipient.

Article 10 【A Change to Remittance Information】
